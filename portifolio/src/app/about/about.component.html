About Hamza

Hamza Mohammed Ausiy, a prominent Tanzanian artist, was born in 1966 on the island of Zanzibar. His early years were immersed in the rich cultural heritage of the region, which has significantly influenced his artistic style. Ausiy's formal education included studying art and design, where he honed his skills and developed his unique perspective.

Artistic Influences

Ausiy's artistic style draws inspiration from a variety of sources. His deep connection to Zanzibar's historical Stone Town is evident in his work, as he often incorporates elements of the island's architecture, people, and landscapes. Additionally, he has been influenced by various artistic movements, including contemporary African art and the broader global art scene.

Creative Process

Ausiy's creative process involves a meticulous approach. He often begins with sketches and studies, capturing the essence of his subjects. His monochromatic palette, predominantly using shades of black and white, is a hallmark of his style. He employs a variety of techniques, including brushwork, texture, and layering, to create depth and emotion in his paintings.
